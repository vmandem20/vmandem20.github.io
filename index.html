Profile

Background

I'm a Food Process Engineering graduate with a research focus that bridges analytical chemistry and food manufacturing. My dissertation applies Diffusion Ordered Spectroscopy (DOSY) NMR to investigate how fatty-acid micelles trap and release flavour compounds in whisky and spirits — the first systematic study of its kind. Alongside this, I've worked on non-thermal preservation (UV-C treatment of milk at CFTRI, Mysuru), food safety and quality systems, and applied product development from waste-stream valorisation to adulteration detection. I'm looking for research assistant, KTP, or PhD opportunities in non-thermal processing, food biotechnology, fermentation and microbiology, or sustainable food manufacturing.

Research

Projects & findings

Investigation of flavour-releasing components in whisky and spirits using DOSY NMR

MSc Dissertation · Heriot-Watt University · Supervisors: Dr David Ellis, Prof. Clare McCabe · 2026

Testing the micellar-trapping hypothesis for volatile flavour compounds — such as guaiacol, the phenol behind peaty, smoky aroma — in fatty-acid/ethanol/water spirit matrices. Preparing binary and ternary model solutions across ethanol concentrations and temperatures, and optimising BPPSTE-LED pulse sequences to suppress convection artefacts in low-viscosity solvents.

METHOD DOSY NMR (BPPSTE-LED) TEMPS 10 / 25 / 40 °C FATTY ACIDS C8:0, C10:0, C12:0 ANALYSIS TOPSpin · DOSY Toolbox · Bland–Altman

Research Intern · CFTRI, Mysuru, India · Dec 2024 – Mar 2025

Applied UV-C pre-treatment to raw cow milk ahead of spray drying, then benchmarked the treated powder against a conventional control across yield, physicochemical, structural, and microbiological parameters.

Value-added cookies from underutilised oil cake

Community-Based Research Project · B.Tech, Food Technology

Formulated cookies from de-fatted peanut and sesame oil-cake flours at four total substitution levels (0/25/50/75%), to valorise a waste stream from oil-expelling industries. Evaluated physical, textural, colour, proximate, microbiological, and sensory quality against a control, using a structured 30-panellist hedonic evaluation and a texture analyser for objective firmness measurement.

SELECTED FORMULATION F3 — 50% total oilcake substitution, 75:25 peanut:sesame ratio TEXTURE (peak load) 21.2 N control → 15.6 N (F3) → 12.4 N (F4) COLOUR (L*, top) 94.6 control → 42.3 (F3) → 58.7 (F4) PROTEIN 4.5% control → 12.97% (F3) FAT 19.8% control → 22.72% (F3) ENERGY 486 kcal (F3) PANEL 30 semi-trained panellists, 9-point hedonic scale

Extraction of starch from bread

Unit Operations Project · B.Tech, Food Technology

Extracted starch from stale, retrograded bread and from raw wheat flour, then compared the functional properties of the two sources to evaluate food-waste valorisation potential.

Home automation system using sensors

IoT Sensors and Devices Module · Team Project (5) · B.Tech, 2022–23

Built an IoT-based energy-efficient fan and light controller. A temperature sensor (LM35) automatically scaled fan speed via PWM as motor heat increased, reducing energy waste; an LDR sensor drove ambient-light-based automatic lighting; and dual IR sensors detected room occupancy to switch the fan and light on or off as people entered or left, with a Bluetooth app for manual override.

COMPONENTS Arduino Uno, LM35, LDR, dual IR sensors, HC-05 Bluetooth CONTROL LOGIC PWM-scaled fan speed, occupancy-based auto-switching

Solar-based drip irrigation system

Clean Energy Design Project · Team Project (5)

Designed a solar-powered, automated drip irrigation system for rooftop and home gardening. A solar panel and rechargeable battery supplied power, a soil moisture sensor triggered an Arduino-controlled solenoid valve, and drip-line piping delivered water only when moisture fell below a set threshold — reducing both electricity and water waste versus manual watering.

POWER Solar panel + rechargeable battery CONTROL Arduino-driven solenoid valve, moisture-threshold triggered DELIVERY Drip line, sub-metre spaced outlets
